## 6. The Amplitude Illusion (Why "Brightness" Fails)

In classical physics, it was assumed that if you shined a very bright, high-amplitude red light at a metal plate, the sheer brute force of the wave would eventually knock an electron loose. When experiments proved this false, classical wave theory broke. The **Universal Plumbing Theory (UPT)** solves this paradox by refactoring the physical definition of "Amplitude" in a superfluid.

If you cannot make *one* wave taller, how do you make a laser beam brighter? **You unspool more knots.**

When a high-amplitude, low-frequency wave (a bright red laser) hits the atom, it fails to eject the electron due to strict spatial geometry.

* **External Shoving:** The few ripples that *do* hit the electron are wound too loosely (low frequency). They hit the outside of the knot out of sync. They can push the knot sideways (Compton Scattering), but they cannot stack pressure *inside* the internal $720^\circ$ circulation to cause a breakout. 

**The Mechanical Verdict:** You cannot crush a tiny gear by throwing a million wrenches at it if only one wrench can physically fit into the housing at a time. High amplitude pushes the doorframe; high frequency picks the lock.
