$$F_{EM} \propto P_{dyn} = \frac{1}{2}\rho c^2$$

## 3. Gravity (Static Pressure Deficit)

However, because the superfluid is incredibly stiff, the kinetic rotation of the core only produces a fractional strain on the surrounding ocean. The gravitational deficit ($\Delta P$) is the dynamic pressure divided by the immense resting stiffness of the medium:
$$F_{Gravity} \propto \Delta P = \frac{P_{dyn}}{P_0}$$

$$\frac{F_{EM}}{F_{Gravity}} = \frac{P_{dyn}}{(P_{dyn} / P_0)} = P_0$$
