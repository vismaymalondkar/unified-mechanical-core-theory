$$A_{hole} = \pi \cdot (R_{torus})^2$$
$$A_{hole} = \pi \cdot (1.972 \times 10^{-13})^2 \approx 1.222 \times 10^{-25} \text{ m}^2$$

$$Q_{max} = A_{hole} \cdot c$$
$$Q_{max} = (1.222 \times 10^{-25} \text{ m}^2) \cdot (2.9979 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})$$
$$Q_{max} \approx \mathbf{3.663 \times 10^{-17} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}}$$

The measured experimental charge of an electron dictates the required flow rate:
$$Q_{req} = e = \mathbf{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}}$$

By dividing the required flow by the maximum limit, we find the particle's Rankine Integration Coefficient ($k$):
$$k = \frac{Q_{req}}{Q_{max}} = \frac{1.602 \times 10^{-19}}{3.663 \times 10^{-17}} \approx \mathbf{0.00437}$$
