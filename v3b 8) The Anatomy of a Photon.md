## **4. The Macroscopic Properties (Elastic Strain)**
Because the photon is an open, elastic hydrodynamic spring, its macroscopic properties (Color and Polarization) are strictly determined by how the ocean physically deforms its shape.

### **A. Longitudinal Strain (Frequency / Color)**
If you apply a pulling or pushing force to the ends of the photon spring, the physical length of the wire ($Mass/Energy$) does not change, but the distance between the individual coils expands or compresses.
* **Redshift:** Gravity pulling backward on the flying spring stretches the coils further apart, increasing physical wavelength ($\lambda$) and decreasing frequency.
* **Blueshift:** Falling into a gravity well compresses the spring front-to-back, packing the coils tighter together.
* *Note: Because the wire is never cut, this longitudinal strain is 100% elastic and reversible.*

### **B. Transverse Geometry (Polarization)**
If stretching the spring changes its color, squashing its cross-sectional profile changes its polarization. This dictates how the front of the spring slices the ocean and leaves a wake.
* **Circular Polarization:** The relaxed spring is a perfect circle. Its helical thread slices the water evenly, leaving a rotating corkscrew wake.
* **Linear Polarization:** When the photon passes through the dense, ordered fluid channels of a crystal lattice, the hydrostatic pressure physically squashes the perfect circle into a flat oval or line. It now slaps the water strictly up-and-down or side-to-side. 
* *Note: Passing this flat spring through a twisting channel (a Waveplate) will physically squeeze it back into a perfect circle. Polarization is a completely reversible hydrodynamic extrusion.*
