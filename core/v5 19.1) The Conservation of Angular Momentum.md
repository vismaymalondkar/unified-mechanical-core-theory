# **Universal Plumbing Theory: The Conservation of Angular Momentum**

In the Universal Plumbing Theory (UPT), substituting $\alpha$ into the open spring equation does not require assuming $r_{coil} = R_{torus}$. It is a strict mechanical requirement dictated by classical fluid kinematics and the Conservation of Angular Momentum (Planck's Constant, $h$).

---

## **1. The Physical Identity of Planck's Constant ($h$)**
In legacy quantum mechanics, $h$ is an abstract unit of "action." In the UPT, $h$ is literal, physical Gyroscopic Angular Momentum. The reduced angular momentum ($\hbar$) of the fundamental fluid wire is calculated using classical kinematics ($L = mvr$):
$$\hbar = \frac{m_e \cdot c \cdot r_{pipe}}{\alpha}$$

Because the Electron's geometric hardware is strictly defined as $\alpha = \frac{r_{pipe}}{R_{torus}}$, we can substitute $\alpha$ to reveal the true mechanical lever arm of the particle's momentum:
$$\hbar = m_e \cdot c \cdot \left(\frac{r_{pipe}}{r_{pipe} / R_{torus}}\right)$$
$$\mathbf{\hbar = m_e \cdot c \cdot R_{torus}}$$
The angular momentum of the resting Electron is exactly the mass of the fluid wire ($m_e$) spinning at the acoustic limit ($c$) at a radial lever arm of $R_{torus}$.

## **2. The Unspooling Event (Matter to Light)**
When an Electron ($0_1$ knot) is severed and unspools into a flying Toroidal spring (a Photon), angular momentum MUST be perfectly conserved within the frictionless superfluid ocean. 

Let us examine the three variables of the photon's angular momentum ($m \cdot v \cdot r_{coil}$):
1. **Mass ($m$):** The total unspooled length of the physical vortex thread does not change during the severing event. ($m_{photon} = m_{electron}$).
2. **Velocity ($v$):** The internal fluid of the Rankine core is permanently choked at Mach 1. ($v = c$).
3. **Radius ($r_{coil}$):** Since the angular momentum ($\hbar$), the mass ($m$), and the velocity ($c$) are permanently locked, the radial lever arm of the spinning mass **cannot change**.

---

## **3. The Geometric Translation**
To satisfy the absolute conservation of momentum, the fluid thread must maintain the exact same radial distance from its center of rotation before and after the topological snap.
* **Matter (Closed Loop):** The center of rotation is the empty donut hole. The radius is $\mathbf{R_{torus}}$.
* **Light (Open Spring):** The center of rotation is the linear axis of flight. The radius is $\mathbf{r_{coil}}$.

If the photon spring attempted to expand or contract its coils as it flew away, it would instantly violate the conservation of angular momentum. Therefore, the universe strictly forces the radius of the open photon's coils to perfectly match the macroscopic radius of the closed electron's track:
$$\mathbf{r_{coil} = R_{torus}}$$

---

**Conclusion:** Because $r_{coil}$ and $R_{torus}$ represent the exact same physical lever arm—just viewed in two different topological states—the ratio of the wire's thickness to its radius remains identical. This is why $\frac{r_{pipe}}{R_{torus}} = \alpha$ perfectly translates to $\frac{r_{pipe}}{r_{coil}} = \alpha$.
