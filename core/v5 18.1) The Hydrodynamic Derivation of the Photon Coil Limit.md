The physical thickness of the vortex thread ($r_{pipe}$) relative to its minimum helical coiling radius ($r_{coil}$) is an indestructible hardware constant locked by the baseline pressure of the universe. This ratio is the Fine Structure Constant:
$$\frac{r_{pipe}}{r_{coil}} = \alpha \approx \frac{1}{137.036}$$

$$Formation\ Limit = \frac{L_{tube}}{D_{coil}} = 4$$
$$L_{tube} = 4 \times (2 r_{coil})$$
$$\mathbf{L_{pipe} = 8 r_{coil}}$$
The universe amputates the flying spring at exactly 8 coiling radii. 

#### 3. The Pythagorean Unrolling (Deriving $N$)
To find how many complete loops ($N$) the spring formed before amputation, we mathematically unroll its helical geometry onto a flat 2D Right Triangle:
* **The Hypotenuse:** The total unspooled length of the amputated wire ($8 r_{coil}$).
* **The Base:** The forward acoustic footprint of one fluid rotation ($2\pi r_{coil}$).
* **The Height:** The lateral space taken up by the physical thickness of the coils stacked together ($N \times 2\pi r_{pipe}$).

We plug this directly into the Pythagorean theorem:
$$(8 r_{coil})^2 = (2\pi r_{coil})^2 + (N \cdot 2\pi r_{pipe})^2$$

Divide the entire equation by $(2\pi r_{coil})^2$ to isolate the ratio:
$$\left(\frac{8}{2\pi}\right)^2 = 1 + \left(N \cdot \frac{r_{pipe}}{r_{coil}}\right)^2$$

Simplify the left side and substitute our universal hardware constant ($\alpha = r_{pipe}/r_{coil}$) on the right side:
$$\left(\frac{4}{\pi}\right)^2 = 1 + (N \cdot \alpha)^2$$

Now, we solve for the exact coil count ($N$):
$$1.6211 = 1 + \left(\frac{N}{137.036}\right)^2$$
$$0.6211 = \left(\frac{N}{137.036}\right)^2$$
$$\sqrt{0.6211} = \frac{N}{137.036}$$
$$0.7881 = \frac{N}{137.036}$$
$$N = 137.036 \times 0.7881$$
$$\mathbf{N = 108.00}$$

**Conclusion:** The classical fluid dynamics of continuous jet pinch-off perfectly truncates the photon wire at $8r_{coil}$, which mathematically guarantees the flying spring contains exactly 108 coils.
