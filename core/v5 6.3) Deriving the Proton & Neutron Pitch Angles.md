## **1. The Proton ($\theta_p$): The Trefoil Bend**
The Proton is fundamentally made of 1836 electron loops braided into 17 Super-Strands, which are then tied into a complex $3_1$ Trefoil knot. To find the macroscopic pitch angle of this knot, we calculate how much "extra" wire is required to bend the braided cable into its folded shape. 

* **$Mass_{base}$ (Unstrained 1836 coiled loops):** 1836 × 0.511 MeV = **938.196 MeV**
* **$Mass_{actual}$ (True resting mass of the knot):** **938.272 MeV**

We apply the Universal Strain Law to solve for the exact physical pitch angle ($\theta_p$) of the Trefoil knot:
$$\cos(\theta_p) = \frac{938.196}{938.272}$$
$$\cos(\theta_p) \approx 0.999919$$
$$\mathbf{\theta_p \approx 0.73^\circ}$$

**The Geometric Result & Phase Cancellation:** The super-cable of the Proton is bent to a sweeping geometric pitch of **0.73°**. Because it is folded into three overlapping lobes, roughly 97.5% of the electric wake generated by this angle crashes into itself and cancels out internally (Topological Phase Cancellation). Only the surviving 2.5% escapes to create the macroscopic $+1e$ charge.

## **2. The Neutron ($\theta_n$): The Helical Torsion**
The Neutron (939.565 MeV) is heavier than the Proton, meaning it contains even more "extra" wire. Its macroscopic charge is strictly zero due to the total topological gridlock of its folded $6_3$ knot. 

To find its pitch angle, we use its true hardware baseline. The Neutron consists of exactly 1837 total loops (the 1836-loop Proton cable + 1 Electron loop). 

* **$Mass_{base}$ (Unstrained 1837 loops):** 1837 × 0.511 MeV = **938.707 MeV**
* **$Mass_{actual}$ (True resting mass of the gridlock):** **939.565 MeV**

We apply the Strain Law to solve for the Neutron's specific pitch angle ($\theta_n$):
$$\cos(\theta_n) = \frac{938.707}{939.565}$$

$$\mathbf{\theta_n \approx 2.45^\circ}$$

**The Geometric Result:** The extreme topological gridlock of the $6_3$ knot forces the Neutron's super-cable to aggressively wrench itself to an angle of **2.45°**. Drawing this extra wire out of the compressible ocean to clear its complex folds requires an additional 0.858 MeV of isentropic strain, establishing its final, heavier mass.
