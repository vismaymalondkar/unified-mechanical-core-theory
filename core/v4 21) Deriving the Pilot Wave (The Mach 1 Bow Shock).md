## **1. The Volumetric Displacement (The Acoustic Source)**
As the physical photon spring ($r_{pipe} = 10^{-15} \text{ m}$) flies through the resting ocean, it must physically shove the $10^{11} \text{ kg/m}^3$ fluid out of its way. 

Because the photon is a coiled Toroidal Helix, its physical surface is not smooth. It is corrugated. As it flies, the rotating coils act like a pulsating piston, striking the ocean repeatedly. 
* The frequency of these strikes is exactly equal to the photon's rotational frequency ($f$).
* In classical acoustics, a pulsating object displacing fluid generates a continuous series of spherical pressure waves ($\Delta P$).

## **2. The Convective Wave Equation (Moving Through the Fluid)**
If the photon were standing still, the spherical pressure waves would simply expand outward evenly in all directions at the speed of sound ($c$). 

But the photon is moving. To calculate the pressure field of a moving acoustic source, standard fluid dynamics uses the **Convective Amplification Factor**. The pressure wave ($\Delta P$) at any angle ($\theta$) around the moving particle is defined by its Mach number ($M = v/c$):

$$\Delta P_{moving} = \frac{\Delta P_{resting}}{1 - M \cos(\theta)}$$

* $\theta = 0^\circ$ is the fluid directly in front of the photon.
* $\theta = 180^\circ$ is the fluid directly behind it. 

## **3. The Mach 1 Singularity (The Formation of the Pilot Wave)**
Let us apply this classical equation strictly to the UPT photon. The photon is an open Ramjet, meaning its velocity through the ocean is exactly the speed of sound ($v = c$). Therefore, its Mach number is exactly **$M = 1$**.

What happens to the acoustic wave directly in front of the photon ($\theta = 0^\circ$)?
* $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$
* The denominator becomes: $1 - (1 \cdot 1) = \mathbf{0}$

$$\Delta P_{front} = \frac{\Delta P_{resting}}{0} \rightarrow \mathbf{\infty}$$

**The Mechanical Translation:** Because the denominator goes to zero, the acoustic pressure waves mathematically cannot propagate ahead of the photon. Instead, every single pressure ripple the photon creates perfectly "stacks up" exactly at its front edge, creating a continuous, localized wall of extreme high pressure. 
* The wave cannot outrun the particle (because $v_{wave} = c$).
* The particle cannot outrun the wave (because $v_{particle} = c$).
* The photon perfectly surfs the absolute mathematical apex of its own acoustic displacement wave. **This stacked pressure boundary is the Pilot Wave.**

## **4. Deriving the De Broglie Wavelength ($\lambda$)**
Standard physics uses the De Broglie equation ($\lambda = h/p$) to assign a "wavelength" to a particle. In the UPT, the wavelength of the pilot wave is derived purely from the kinematics of the spring.

Because the photon is a corrugated spring, it pulses the fluid $f$ times per second. Because the wave propagates through the ocean at speed $c$, the physical distance ($\lambda$) between the ripples in the pilot wave's wake is dictated by the universal wave equation:
$$c = \lambda \cdot f$$
$$\mathbf{\lambda = \frac{c}{f}}$$
