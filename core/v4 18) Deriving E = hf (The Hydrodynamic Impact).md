* It is the invariant "Rotational Horsepower" of a single coil.

## **3. The Collision (Deriving $E = hf$)**
Imagine a photon spring flying through the ocean and crashing head-on into an electron knot (like in the Photoelectric Effect). How much total energy ($E$) does the spring transfer to the electron?

In classical mechanics, Total Power Delivered = (Energy per Impact) $\times$ (Number of Impacts).

Let's plug in the UPT physical definitions:
* **Energy per Impact:** The kinetic rotational energy of a single $360^\circ$ fluid coil (Planck's Constant, $h$).

Therefore, the total kinetic energy delivered by the flying spring is:
$$\mathbf{Total Energy = (Energy of One Coil) \times (Coils per Second)}$$
$$\mathbf{E = h \cdot f}$$
