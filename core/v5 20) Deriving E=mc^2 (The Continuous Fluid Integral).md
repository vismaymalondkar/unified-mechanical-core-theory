## **1. The Compressible Gradient Field ($v$ and $\rho$)**

* **The Velocity Gradient:** The velocity ($v$) of the fluid at any given radius ($r$) decays outward from the $c$-velocity core:
$$v(r) = c \cdot \frac{r_{core}}{r}$$
* **The Density Gradient:** As the fluid accelerates toward Mach 1 near the core, it physically stretches. The density ($\rho$) drops from the resting ocean baseline ($\rho_0$) down to the Critical Density ($\rho^*$) at the throat. Therefore, density is strictly a function of radius: $\rho(r)$.

We cannot calculate the vortex as a single bulk object. We must look at the **Local Energy Density** at any infinitesimal point within the pipe. At any radius $r$, the total local energy is the sum of its internal Kinetic Energy ($e_k$) and the Isentropic Acoustic Strain Energy ($e_p$) required to stretch the ambient ocean:
* **Local Kinetic Energy:** $e_k = \frac{1}{2}\rho(r) v(r)^2$
* **Local Acoustic Strain:** In compressible flow, the potential energy stored in the stretched fluid perfectly mirrors the kinetic energy required to stretch it: $e_p = \frac{1}{2}\rho(r) v(r)^2$

Add them together, and the exact halves form a whole. At every single microscopic point inside the particle, the total energy density is strictly:
$$e_{total} = \rho(r) \cdot v(r)^2$$
