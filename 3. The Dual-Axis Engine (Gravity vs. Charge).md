As the fluid circulates around the tube of the Toroidal knot, it generates immense centrifugal force. Even though the fluid is locked in a Rankine vortex gradient, its rotational inertia physically "flings" fluid radially outward against the surrounding high-density ocean. 
* **The Motion:** The radial, outward-pushing centrifugal exhaust of the spinning pipe. 
* **The Result:** This creates a spherical, isotropic shell of high static pressure radiating away from the knot. We measure this outward hydraulic pumping as **Electric Charge**.

## 2. Gravity: The Bernoulli Sink (Inward Suction)
Gravity is driven by the absolute velocity of the vortex core—the **Hydrostatic Pressure Deficit ($\Delta P$)**.

While the centrifugal force flings fluid outward, the velocity of the spin itself hacks the static pressure of the medium. At the microscopic center of the Rankine vortex (the redline core), the fluid is moving at the absolute acoustic speed limit ($c$). According to **Bernoulli’s Principle**, as fluid velocity spikes, static pressure must drop. 
* **The Motion:** The tangential, $c$-velocity shearing of the core slicing through the medium.
* **The Result:** The extreme speed at the core creates a massive, localized void in the resting universal pressure ($P_0$). The surrounding high-pressure ocean continuously shoves fluid (and other knots) inward to crush this low-pressure void. We measure this inward universal push as **Gravity**.
