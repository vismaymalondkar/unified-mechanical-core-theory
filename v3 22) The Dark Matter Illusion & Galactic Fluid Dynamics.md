$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

Because distance ($r$) is in the denominator, stars at the outer edge of a galaxy should orbit significantly slower than stars near the core. However, observational data shows a **Flat Rotation Curve**. Stars at the galactic rim orbit at the exact same blazing velocity as stars much closer to the center.
