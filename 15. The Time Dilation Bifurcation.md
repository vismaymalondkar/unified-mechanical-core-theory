## 3. Mechanism A: Gravitational Time Dilation (The Denominator Hack)
*Environment: A particle falling into a Black Hole or deep gravity well.*

### The Fluid Dynamics
In deep space, the universal fluid is mostly stationary, meaning static crushing pressure ($P_0$) is at its maximum. Inside a gravity well, the bulk fluid is rushing inward. According to **Bernoulli's Principle**, as fluid velocity increases, static pressure drops. Therefore, the $P_0$ crushing force on the particle weakens.

### The Geometric Result (Volume Expansion)
To prevent the particle from collapsing, its internal fluid constantly spins at $c$, creating an outward centrifugal force. When the external $P_0$ drops, this constant outward force pushes the walls of the particle outward. The Toroidal loop physically swells, increasing its radius ($R$). 

### The Time Output
* **Internal Speed ($u$):** Remains locked at $c$.
* **Track Length ($2\pi R$):** Gets significantly longer.
* **Result:** Because the track is longer, the $c$-speed fluid takes more time to complete a single rotation. **The clock slows down.**

### The Physical Footprint
* **Mass/Gravity:** Because the loop's volume increases, it displaces more of the $10^{10}$ density fluid. The resting mass of the particle physically increases.
* **EM Field:** The electromagnetic acoustic pulsation becomes symmetrical but stretches into a lower frequency (Gravitational Redshift).

## 4. Mechanism B: Velocity Time Dilation (The Numerator Hack)
*Environment: A particle fired from an accelerator at $0.99c$.*

$$u^2 + v^2 = c^2$$

### The Time Output
* **Track Length ($2\pi R$):** Remains exactly at its baseline size.
* **Internal Speed ($u$):** Physically slows down to avoid the Mach 1 structural limit.
* **Result:** Because the internal fluid is moving slower across the same distance, it takes more time to complete a rotation. **The clock slows down.**

### The Physical Footprint

However, the extreme forward velocity ($v$) against the dense ocean creates colossal Ram Pressure (Hydrodynamic Drag, $F_d \propto v^2$). This is observed as "Relativistic Mass"—the particle feels exponentially harder to push.
