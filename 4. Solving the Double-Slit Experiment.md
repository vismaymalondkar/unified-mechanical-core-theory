## 4. The "Observer Effect" Explained (No Consciousness Required)
The greatest mystery of the Double-Slit experiment is that placing a detector at the slits to observe the electron causes the interference pattern to disappear, making the electron act like a classical bullet. Standard physics attributes this to the "collapse of the wavefunction."

* Without its pilot-wave to create an interference pattern on the other side of the slits, the Soliton is no longer steered. It simply flies straight through the slit and hits the wall directly behind it, exactly like a classical particle.
